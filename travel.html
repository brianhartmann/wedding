Estes Park sits at the entrance of Rocky Mountain National Park. From Denver International Airport, the drive to Estes Park is under two hours, which makes it a popular travel destination. This town is the epitome of Colorado charm, and its quaintness adds to its greatness. Because it’s more rustic than a big city, there aren’t many large hotels that could hold a big room block. Below, you’ll find a variety of lodging options to explore – all within a 10-minute drive of our venue.
The Stanley Hotel
This historic hotel is the perfect combination of rustic and luxury. During Stephen King’s stay at the Stanley Hotel, he developed the idea for his famous novel, The Shining. This hotel has quality dining options and iconic views — and even a “ghost tour” of the property if you have extra time to explore. The Lodge at the Stanley is where we suggest you look first.
Rams Horn Village Resort
Described as a “home away from home,” this resort offers cabins that sleep up to six people — equipped with full kitchens. From volleyball courts to gas (and charcoal) grills to bicycles, Rams Horn has an extensive list of amenities. Plus, it’s just up the road from our wedding venue. However, a three-night minimum is required to stay at this resort.
Appenzell Inn
The Appenzell Inn takes an Alpine twist on the classic motel. Located in downtown Estes Park, guests are within walking distance of cute shops and restaurants. This inn has budget-friendly rooms for couples and families. It’s also the place where an eight-year-old Nicole and her family stayed on their first visit to Estes!
Blue Door Inn
As an affordable option in Estes Park, the Blue Door Inn is comfortable, clean, and cozy. It has hundreds of happy reviews praising its extensive amenities and kind-hearted staff. This hotel offers a complimentary breakfast for guests who need a quick bite before hitting the town (or the trails).
